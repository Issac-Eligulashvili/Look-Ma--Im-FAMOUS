ISAAC NEWTON

The man that Revolutionized Physics

Sir Isaac Newton, a towering figure in the scientific revolution of the 17th century, made groundbreaking contributions to physics, mathematics, and astronomy.

Early Years

Sir Isaac Newton, was born in 1643 in Woolsthorpe, England. 3 months after his birth, Newton's father would die, leaving him in the care of his mother and grandmother. Due to his poor upbringing, he would only be able to attend the Free Grammar School in Grantham.

Newton didn't accel in school however, he would still attend Trinity College Cambridge with the hopes of studying law. The bubonic plague would occur at this time, forcing Newton into isolation within his home for 2 years.

Accomplishments

Sir Isaac Newton would make groundbreaking discoveries in optics during his time in isolation during the late 17th century. He would encapsulate all his work in a book titled "Optiks" published in 1704. WIthin this book, Newton would describe his theory on diffraction and how white light is comprised of a spectrum of colors; each with a different wave length. He would carry out his experiments using sunlight and a prism and then observing the color separation that would occur. These experiments would lay the foundation for the understanding of the nature of color. Also, his research of optics would propose the theory that light is made up of particles, laying the groundwork for modern physics.
