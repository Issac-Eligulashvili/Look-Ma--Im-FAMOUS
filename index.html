The man that Revolutionized Physics

Sir Isaac Newton, a towering figure in the scientific revolution of the 17th century, made groundbreaking contributions to physics, mathematics, and astronomy.

Early Years

Sir Isaac Newton, was born in 1643 in Woolsthorpe, England. 3 months after his birth, Newton's father would die, leaving him in the care of his mother and grandmother. Due to his poor upbringing, he would only be able to attend the Free Grammar School in Grantham.

Newton didnt accel in school however, he would still attend Trinity College Cambridge with the hopes of studying law. The bubonic plague would occur at this time, forcing Newton into isolation within his home for 2 years.
